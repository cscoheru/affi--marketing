## 📌 模板 3: 使用问题类

### 目标问题类型
```
"How do I clean my coffee machine?"
"Why does my coffee taste bitter?"
"What's the best way to store coffee beans?"
```

### 回答模板
```
**For coffee storage:**

After researching this, the short answer is: **Don't freeze your beans for
daily use.**

Here's why:

**Freezer issues:**
- Temperature fluctuations cause condensation
- Moisture ruins flavor and causes mold
- Freezer smells transfer to beans
- Oils can degrade

**Better approach:**
1. Buy beans in quantities you'll use in 2-3 weeks
2. Store in airtight container (opaque if possible)
3. Keep in cool, dark place (counter is fine)
4. Avoid direct sunlight and heat

**Bean lifespan:**
- Peak freshness: 1-2 weeks after roasting
- Still good: 3-4 weeks
- Noticeable staleness: 5+ weeks

If you MUST buy in bulk (sales, etc.):
- Freeze the extra in truly airtight bags
- Take out only what you'll use in a week
- Don't refreeze (repeated temp changes = bad)

I wrote up the full guide on coffee storage with more detail:

[Medium Article URL - How to Store Coffee Beans]

The TL;DR: Airtight container on the counter is best for most people.
Freezer is okay for long-term bulk storage but not daily use.
```
