# Why I Regret Buying the De'Longhi ECAM 22.110 After 3 Months

> **Spoiler: I don't actually regret it. But there are things I wish I knew before dropping $350 on this machine.**

---

## Three Months Ago: The Excitement

I'll be honest — I got sucked in by the Amazon reviews. 4.2 stars, thousands of positive reviews, people saying this machine "changed their morning routine."

I was picturing myself as a home barista, crafting perfect espresso with the push of a button. No more $6 daily coffee runs. No more waiting in line. Just me, my De'Longhi, and coffee heaven.

The reality? Well, it's been… complicated.

---

## The Good Stuff (Because There Is Plenty)

Let's start with what this machine actually does well, because honestly? It's pretty impressive.

### It Makes Good Coffee

Not "I can't believe this came from a machine" good. But "this is actually enjoyable" good.

- **The espresso has real crema** — that golden foam on top that makes it look legit
- **Temperature is spot on** — hot but not burn-your-tongue hot
- **Consistent results** — every cup tastes the same (in a good way)

### It's Stupid Easy to Use

I am not a morning person. I barely function before caffeine. This machine gets that.

- **Push one button** — literally
- **It grinds, tamps, brews** — automatically
- **Coffee in under a minute** — no patience required

The controls are big, clear, and actually make sense. No tiny buttons or confusing menus. You want espresso? Press the espresso button. You want a bigger cup? Press the coffee button. Done.

### The Built-in Grinder is Actually Good

I was skeptical about the built-in grinder. Usually, all-in-one machines compromise on everything.

But this one?
- **13 grind settings** — more than enough adjustment
- **Doesn't make a huge mess** — surprisingly contained
- **Quiet enough** — won't wake up the whole house

### The Milk Frother Works

I'm no latte art master (okay, my foam art looks like失败的 clouds), but:
- **Steam wand gets hot enough** — no problem there
- **Froth is decent** — not cafe quality, but pretty good for home
- **Easy to clean** — comes apart easily

## The Not-So-Good Stuff (What I Wish I Knew)

Here's the stuff that nobody talks about in the reviews. The stuff that would've made me pause before buying.

### The Bean Feeding Problem (This Is Real, People)

Remember how I said the grinder works well? It does — until it doesn't.

**The issue:** Beans stop falling into the grinder.

**The reality:** Every morning, I have to grab the little brush they give you and stir the beans so they'll actually drop into the grinder.

**How bad is it?** Annoying, but not a dealbreaker. It takes 5 seconds, but it happens literally every day.

**The workaround:** Don't use super oily beans (looking at you, Starbucks). Dry-roasted beans seem to flow better.

### No Timer, No Delay Start

This is baffling to me. It's 2024. Even my cheap coffee maker had a timer.

This machine?
- **No programmable start time**
- **No "ready at 6am" feature**
- **Manual operation only**

Meaning: I wake up, stumble to the kitchen, turn it on, and wait. And wait. And wait some more.

It's not terrible (warm-up takes maybe 2 minutes), but for a $350 machine? Seems like a weird oversight.

### The "Can Only Use Our Descaler" Thing

Here's something I didn't expect: **you have to buy De'Longhi's brand descaler.**

It's not that expensive (like $15 on Amazon), but:
- **Can't use vinegar** (they explicitly say don't)
- **Can't use generic descaler** (voids warranty?)
- **Have to buy their stuff** (always convenient, right?)

It's not a huge cost, but it feels like being locked into their ecosystem.

---

## The Weird Design Choices

A few things that make me go "huh?"

### The Grinder Dial Thing

The grinder adjustment is on a dial, which seems normal. But here's the catch: **you can only adjust it while the grinder is running.**

Why? Because adjusting it while stationary can supposedly damage it.

Is this true? I don't know. But I'm not brave enough to test it and find out.

### The Water Tank Position

The water tank is at the back right, kind of tucked away. It's not terrible, but:
- **Hard to see the water level**
- **Awkward to remove**
- **Just… slightly annoying every time**

It works, but it's not exactly thoughtful design.

### The Pre-Ground Coffee "Feature"

You can use pre-ground coffee instead of beans! Great feature, right?

**Except:** You can only add one scoop at a time. One scoop = one cup.

Want to make two cups with pre-ground? Scoop, brew, wait, scoop again, brew.

Who is this for? People who only ever drink one cup? People who hate convenience?

I don't get it, but at least the option exists.

## Three Months In: Would I Buy It Again?

**Honestly? Yeah.**

But I'd manage my expectations differently.

**What I'd tell my past self:**

1. **This machine makes good coffee, not great coffee** — adjust your expectations accordingly
2. **You'll have to stir the beans every morning** — it's annoying but normal
3. **No timer means no waking up to ready coffee** — set your alarm 5 minutes earlier
4. **It's not "set it and forget it"** — you'll need to maintain it regularly
5. **For $350, it's decent value** — but it's not life-changing

**The verdict:** It's a solid machine for the price. It does what it's supposed to do, makes good coffee, and doesn't require a PhD to operate.

But if you're looking for cafe-quality espresso? This isn't it. If you want perfect convenience? This isn't it either.

It's somewhere in the middle — a pretty good machine for regular people who want better-than-average coffee without the fuss.

---

## So Who Is This Actually For?

**Buy the De'Longhi ECAM 22.110 if you:**
- Want automated coffee without the crazy price tag
- Don't need latte art perfection
- Are okay with some daily quirks (bean stirring, manual on)
- Want a machine that learns your preferences
- Have $350 to spend and don't want to think too hard

**Skip it if you:**
- Want cafe-quality espresso
- Need programmable features (timer, delayed start)
- Drink nothing but oily dark-roast beans
- Expect zero maintenance
- Are a coffee snob (you know who you are)

---

## The Bottom Line

After three months, I'm mostly happy with my De'Longhi. It makes good coffee reliably, doesn't require a barista degree to operate, and was reasonably priced.

Do I wish I had researched more? Maybe. Would I have chosen differently? Probably not.
